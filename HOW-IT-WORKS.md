## What happens when someone calls your business

Let's walk through a real scenario. Sarah is a plumber in Canberra. Her water heater customer, Mark, calls at 7pm on a Saturday. Sarah is at her daughter's birthday party.

1. **Mark dials Sarah's business number.** The call rings into Twilio (the phone network we use), which immediately tells our system "there's a call coming in for Sarah's Plumbing".

2. **Kestrel answers within one ring.** "Thanks for calling Sarah's Plumbing, this is Kestrel — how can I help?" She sounds like a real receptionist because she IS speaking, just generated by an AI in real time.

3. **Mark explains his hot water system is leaking.** Kestrel asks the right follow-up questions — when did it start, is it spraying water everywhere, do you need someone tonight or can it wait till morning?

4. **Kestrel checks Sarah's calendar.** She knows Sarah has booked appointments tomorrow morning. She offers Mark a 9am slot. He accepts.

5. **Mark gives his name, phone, and address.** Kestrel reads them back to confirm. Booking is locked into Sarah's Cal.com calendar.

6. **Sarah's phone buzzes.** She gets a text: *"New booking — Mark Henderson, leaking hot water system, 12 Banks St Belconnen, tomorrow 9am. Phone 0412 345 678."* She looks at it, smiles, goes back to her daughter.

7. **Sarah arrives at 9am the next day.** Mark answers the door. He doesn't even realise he never spoke to a human last night — only that he called the plumber at 7pm on a Saturday and got an answer, a time, and a confirmation.

**The whole thing took 90 seconds.** Without Kestrel, that call would have gone to voicemail. Mark probably would have tried two more plumbers and booked with whoever picked up. Sarah would have lost a $400 job and not even known.

## Where your data lives

This is the question every small business owner should ask, and most AI vendors dodge.

**Everything runs on Cloudflare.** Cloudflare is the same company that protects 20% of the internet — banks, government sites, big retailers. Our infrastructure runs on their global network, with data physically stored in Australian data centres.

**What we store:**
- Call transcripts and basic call metadata (caller phone, time, duration), so you can review what happened
- Voicemails left for you (audio + a written transcript)
- Lead details captured by your chatbot (name, phone, email, what they enquired about)
- Booking records (who, when, what for)
- Customer profiles for callers who phone you more than once, so Kestrel can recognise them and reference past conversations

**What we DON'T store:**
- Credit card numbers (we never collect them — payments happen in your existing systems)
- Anything you don't want us to (just tell us)
- Recordings older than 30 days (auto-deleted)
- Anything we sell or share with a third party (we never do)

**Who can access your data:**
- You (via your admin dashboard)
- Jarek (LayerOps' founder) for support and debugging
- Cloudflare's infrastructure (encrypted at rest, never read by humans)
- Anthropic (the AI provider) sees the text of conversations to generate responses, but they don't store it for training

**If you ever leave LayerOps:** we export everything we have on you and delete it within seven days. No "data hostage" situation. No fee.

## What we do and don't do with AI

**What the AI does:**
- Generates the actual words Kestrel says to your customers, in real time
- Reads incoming messages and decides what to respond
- Decides when to ask a follow-up question vs when to book the appointment vs when to take a callback
- Recognises returning callers and references their history

**What the AI does NOT do:**
- Make legal, medical, or financial decisions (Kestrel is trained to say "I'm not the right person for that — let me get Jarek/your dentist/your accountant to call you back")
- Make up information about your business (it only knows what you've told us)
- Promise anything you didn't approve (no fake quotes, no fake availability)
- Replace you for the actual work — it just handles the stuff that comes BEFORE the actual work

**What happens if the AI gets confused:** It asks clarifying questions, and if it still can't help, it falls back to "Let me get Jarek to call you back — what's the best number?" and takes a message. It never invents information or pretends to know something it doesn't.

Google Business Profile: The Free Tool Every Canberra Tradie Ignores

Walk down any street in Canberra and you'll see tradies working hard — plumbers in Civic, electricians in Belconnen, painters in Tuggeranong. But here's the thing that gets me: most of these skilled professionals are invisible online when potential customers are searching for help.

There's a free tool that could put them in front of every person in their area searching for their services. It's called Google Business Profile (used to be called Google My Business), and most tradies either ignore it completely or set it up wrong.

Why Your Google Business Profile Actually Matters

When someone in Canberra has a blocked drain or needs their deck repainted, what do they do? They grab their phone and search "plumber near me" or "painter Canberra". If you don't have a properly set up Google Business Profile, you won't show up in those results. Simple as that.

I've seen plumbers in Gungahlin lose jobs to competitors simply because the competitor had a complete profile with recent photos and reviews, while they had nothing. The customer couldn't even find their phone number without clicking through to a website that took forever to load.

Your Google Business Profile appears in three key places: Google Search results, Google Maps, and when people search for businesses like yours in your area. Miss this, and you're missing customers who are ready to pay for your services right now.

The Mistakes Most Canberra Tradies Make

I've audited dozens of local businesses, and the same mistakes keep popping up:

  • Incomplete information: Missing phone numbers, wrong addresses, or no business hours listed
  • No photos: Would you hire a painter who won't show you their work?
  • Ignoring reviews: Not responding to reviews (good or bad) makes you look like you don't care
  • Wrong category: Listed as "contractor" instead of "electrician" means you won't show up for specific searches
  • Old information: Still showing the mobile number from 2019 that you don't use anymore

How to Set Up Your Profile Properly

Setting up your Google Business Profile properly takes about 30 minutes, but it can bring in customers for years. Here's what actually matters:

Complete every field: Business name, address, phone number, website, business hours, and a detailed description of what you do. If you're a plumber in Queanbeyan, say that clearly — don't just write "home services".

Choose the right category: Pick the most specific category that matches your main service. "Plumber" beats "contractor" every time for plumbing searches.

Add quality photos: Upload at least 10 photos showing your work, your van with signage, and yourself on the job. People want to see who they're hiring and what quality of work to expect.

Get your first reviews: Ask your last few happy customers to leave a review. Even three genuine reviews make a huge difference in how trustworthy you appear.

The Local Advantage

Here's something many Canberra tradies don't realise: Google loves local businesses. When someone searches for services, Google prioritises businesses close to them. An electrician in Dickson will often outrank a larger company in Sydney for "electrician near me" searches from Canberra residents.

This is your advantage. You don't need to compete with big national companies — you just need to be the best option in your local area. A complete Google Business Profile helps Google understand exactly where you are and what you do.

Keep It Updated and Active

Setting up your profile is just the start. The businesses that get the most leads treat their Google Business Profile like a living thing:

  • Post photos of recent jobs (weekly if possible)
  • Respond to every review within a few days
  • Update your hours during holidays or busy periods
  • Add new services as you expand your business

I know a carpenter in Fyshwick who posts a photo of every major job he completes. His profile is full of fresh content, and he consistently gets calls from people who found him through Google searches.

The Bottom Line

Your Google Business Profile is like a free shopfront that's open 24/7. Every day you don't have it set up properly, potential customers are finding your competitors instead of you. The fix takes less time than a typical service call, but the impact lasts for years.
